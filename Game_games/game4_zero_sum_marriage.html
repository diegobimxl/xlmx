BIAS REVEALED: Sunk Cost Fallacy

You kept investing in a deteriorating relationship not because it was working — but because you already had so much in it. The sunk cost fallacy: past investment justified future sacrifice, even when the math didn't. Every "one more repair" felt rational in the moment. The energy spent recovering what was already lost was the real cost.
