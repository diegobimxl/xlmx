BIAS REVEALED: Endowment Effect & Loss Aversion

You felt the tax as a personal attack. The loopholes felt urgent not because they were rational — but because the assets felt like an extension of yourself. This is the Endowment Effect: we value what we own more than its market price. Loss Aversion doubled the pain: losing $200K felt twice as bad as gaining $200K felt good.
